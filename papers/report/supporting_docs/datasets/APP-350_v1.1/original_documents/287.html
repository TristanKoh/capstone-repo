Omni Swipe Terms of Service and Privacy Policy

Omni Swipe requests certain permissions from your Android device and collects certain information from you as a user to provide specific functionalities and conveniences. We understand your privacy is important to you and this document explains why Omni Swipe needs the permissions it requests and how we collect, use, and protect your information.

I. Permissions Requested

Below is an explanation of the various permission types requested and used by Omni Swipe:

1.Directly call

Used in the "Interact" tab so you can tap the phone icon for a contact immediately call them in one step instead of two.

2.Contacts and notifications

Used for showing contact information and notifications.

3.Network and WiFi

Used for turning on/off your network and internet connection in the Toolbox tab.

4.Bluetooth

Used for turning on/off your Bluetooth connection in the Toolbox tab.

5.Camera

Used for launching the camera application in the Toolbox tab.

6.Flashlight

Used for turning on/off the flashlight application in the Toolbox tab.

7.Wake and Lock

Used to allow the flashlight to persist when turned on from the Toolbox tab.

8.Get tasks

Used for showing your most recently-used applications in the Recents tab.

9.Kill background processes

Used for the Booster feature in the Toolbox tab.

10.Phone state

Used to prevent Omni Swipe from being activated in the calling interface.

11.SMS

Used to show text message content in the Interact tab.

12.Receive boot completed

Used to automatically launch Omni Swipe upon boot. If Omni Swipe does not work after restarting your phone, please check that this permission is enabled.

13.System alert window

Used to turn on floating window support required for Omni Swipe's functionality. At present, only MI and Huawei devices need this to be manually enabled, which we provide guides for. If your phone also needs this permission to be manually set, please contact us immediately.

14.Settings

Used to modify various mobile device settings in the Toolbox tab, such as Volume, Brightness, etc.
